THE LAST MESSAGE

Based on True Events - October 31st, 2024

Sarah had always been careful about online privacy. She used VPNs, encrypted messaging, and never gave out personal information. But on Halloween night, something changed.

It started with a notification at 3:47 AM. Her phone buzzed on the nightstand, pulling her from a dreamless sleep. The screen glowed in the darkness, showing a message from an unknown number.

[03:47:23] UNKNOWN
I can see you sleeping.

Sarah's heart stopped. She lived alone on the fifth floor of a secure apartment building. The blinds were closed. She grabbed her phone with trembling hands and checked every window—all locked, curtains drawn. Probably just a prank, she thought. Some automated spam message with unfortunate timing.

She blocked the number and tried to go back to sleep. But fifteen minutes later, another buzz.

[04:03:11] UNKNOWN
Blocking me won't help. Nice blue sheets, by the way.

The sheets WERE blue. Sarah's blood ran cold. She jumped out of bed and checked her laptop—camera covered. Phone camera—she never gave permissions. Smart TV—unplugged months ago. How could anyone possibly know?

She called the police, but they said without an explicit threat, there was little they could do. They suggested she stay with a friend. Sarah packed a bag, hands shaking, and headed for the door.

Another message stopped her in the hallway.

[04:31:47] UNKNOWN
Where are you going, Sarah? Your friend Michelle won't answer. She's sleeping too deeply tonight.

Sarah never told anyone she was going to Michelle's house. She hadn't even called her yet. With trembling fingers, she tried Michelle's number. It went straight to voicemail. She tried again. And again.

The elevator dinged behind her. Empty. But the phone buzzed again.

[04:33:02] UNKNOWN
Wrong direction. I'm not in the elevator.

Sarah spun around, looking at every shadow, every corner. The hallway was empty except for the flickering fluorescent light at the far end. She ran back to her apartment, slammed the door, and pushed her dresser against it.

She called 911 again. As she spoke to the operator, explaining everything, a final message arrived. This one was different. It wasn't text.

It was a photo.

Of her. Right now. Talking on the phone. In her apartment. Taken from inside her bedroom closet.

⚠ POLICE REPORT #HC-103124 - SUBJECT REMAINS MISSING ⚠

When police arrived at 5:02 AM, they found the apartment empty. The dresser was still against the door. All windows remained locked from the inside. Sarah's phone was on the bed, camera lens shattered, still displaying that final photograph.

But Sarah was gone.

The closet door stood open, revealing nothing but empty hangers swaying slightly, as if someone had just brushed past them. On the closet floor, they found Sarah's shoes, placed neatly side by side.

She never wore shoes indoors.
